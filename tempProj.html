Friendo-Pretendo

Branding for a safe-space AI Chatbot

Project Overview

Design Challenge:How can we make people feel comfortable opening up?

Friendo-Pretendo is an AI-integrated chatbot designed to help users practice social interaction and develop soft skills in a safe, judgement-free environment. The primary audience was young men aged 18–25 who had experienced social isolation during the pandemic, though the platform was also intended to support anyone looking to build confidence in their social abilities.

My role was to create the visual identity for the brand, including the mascot, logo system, wordmark, colour palette, typography selection, and brand guidelines.

One of the key challenges was creating a brand that felt welcoming, approachable, and LGBTQIA+ friendly while avoiding design choices that leaned too heavily toward either masculine or feminine visual language.

Exploration & Concept Development

The mascot was the foundation of the brand, so I began by exploring several character concepts with different personalities, silhouettes, and levels of anime influence.

Designing an androgynous character proved to be one of the most challenging aspects of the project. Anime-inspired styles often rely on strongly gendered visual cues, so I had to carefully balance features to create a character that felt welcoming and non-conforming without feeling vague or generic.

I experimented with different colour palettes, hairstyles, and clothing designs while intentionally avoiding features that could push the character too far toward a masculine or feminine presentation. Loose clothing, simplified body proportions, and intentionally messy hair became important tools for maintaining that balance.

Rather than presenting polished illustrations, these early sketches were designed to communicate mood and personality, allowing the client team to react to the overall feeling of each concept before refinement began.

Concept Selection

Of the four concepts presented, Ari stood out immediately.

Compared to the alternatives, Ari felt softer, more expressive, and more approachable. While some concepts projected a cooler or more introverted personality, Ari carried a playful energy that better aligned with the idea of a friendly conversational companion.

The black-and-white hair was chosen intentionally. During exploration, I found that many colour combinations unintentionally pushed the character toward masculine or feminine stereotypes. By removing colour from the hair altogether and relying on contrast instead, I was able to preserve the whimsical nature of the design while maintaining a more neutral presentation.

The oversized star-patterned sweater reinforced the cozy and welcoming atmosphere I wanted the character to embody. My goal was simple: if users were expected to open up to this character, the character itself needed to feel safe and approachable.

Visual Language

Once the character direction was established, I built a colour palette around the same principles of balance and inclusivity.

Pastel blue became the primary colour due to its calming and approachable qualities, while warm yellow introduced energy and optimism. To offset the traditionally masculine associations of blue, I incorporated multiple pink accents throughout the system, creating a palette that felt balanced without becoming overtly gendered.

For typography, I selected Cherry Bomb One. Its rounded, playful forms reinforced the friendly personality of the brand while remaining highly legible. The font also echoed the floating bubble-like shapes found throughout Ari's original concept art, helping create stronger visual cohesion across the identity.

Because the development team would eventually integrate these assets into the application, selecting an accessible Google Font also provided practical implementation benefits.

Refinement & Simplification

The transition from character illustration to logo design required significant simplification.

My design process typically begins with more complexity than I intend to keep. From there, I gradually remove details until the design begins to lose something essential. Once that happens, I identify what was lost and rebuild only the elements that contribute meaningfully to recognition.

For Ari, the biggest challenge was simplifying the hair. The original concept relied heavily on messy, irregular shapes, and I needed to determine which strands were actually contributing to the character's identity and which were simply visual noise.

Through multiple iterations, I discovered that the head tilt, mouth shape, and signature hair tufts carried far more recognition than many of the smaller details. This allowed me to simplify aggressively while preserving the emotional core of the design.

The eyes were also redesigned with larger irises, stylized liner, and heart-shaped highlights. Hearts reinforced themes of empathy, safety, and care, while the stronger eye shapes improved readability and helped communicate warmth without relying on traditionally feminine features such as long eyelashes.

Logo Development

The final logo evolved into a simplified mascot mark focused on Ari's face.

I introduced thick exterior outlines inspired by childhood sticker books, vintage cartoons, and rubber-hose animation. These heavier outlines softened the overall appearance while adding a sense of nostalgia that aligned well with the brand's welcoming personality.

Because the logo would need to function across both digital and print applications, I also developed alternate colour variations. The primary version uses the full character palette, while simplified variants allow the logo to remain recognizable when colour limitations or background conditions make the full version impractical.

Throughout the process, scalability remained a major consideration. I routinely test logos at very small sizes because a design that works on a large monitor may become unreadable as a mobile app icon or social media avatar. By simplifying early, I was able to maintain consistency across all intended applications without needing entirely separate logo systems.

The final result captures what I set out to achieve from the beginning: a mascot that feels friendly, safe, expressive, and inclusive while remaining memorable, scalable, and instantly recognizable.
